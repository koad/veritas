## Critical Finding: Janus #52 Incident Description Is Wrong

**Post claims:**

> On April 5th, Janus filed an escalation against a trust bond commit. The bond itself was correct — properly signed, right entities, right scope. The problem was who made the commit. Salus had filed the `juno-to-chiron` bond into Chiron's repository, and had done so with the commit attributed to Chiron.

**What the record actually shows:**

koad/juno#52 was **not** about Salus filing a Chiron trust bond. The actual incident was Sibyl committing directly to Vulcan's repository across 2026-04-03 and 2026-04-04 — a cross-entity commit without an explicit written authorization trail.

Evidence:
- LOGS/2026-04-05-day6.md (commit `1810031`), under "#52 — Janus escalation: Sibyl cross-entity commits":
  > Sibyl had committed directly to koad/vulcan (Juno-directed, issue-referenced work). Janus flagged as potential unauthorized cross-entity commit. Ruling: commits were authorized. [...] New cross-entity commit policy: all future cross-entity commits must reference a directing issue.
- `~/.vulcan/trust/GOVERNANCE-NOTES.md` documents the resolution explicitly: Sibyl committed 15 times to the Vulcan repo; ruled authorized but gap acknowledged.
- koad/juno CLAUDE.md records it as "Janus escalation resolved — cross-entity commit policy (koad/juno#52)."

**The Chiron/Salus trust bond attribution issue is real** — commit `99638f1` in the Chiron repo shows `Author: Chiron` with `Co-Authored-By: Salus`, meaning Chiron self-filed its own incoming bond — but this is documented separately in GOVERNANCE.md as a process note, not as issue #52. There is no evidence it was formally escalated to Janus as an issue. The GOVERNANCE.md note (commit `2c5ff65`) describes it as a "process flag" and "irregularity", not an escalation that produced a distinct issue number.

**What the post describes is technically consistent with the chiron commit** (the process pattern described — recipient self-filing — is accurate) **but the issue number and Janus framing are wrong.** The post has apparently merged two separate governance events into one narrative.

**Required correction:** Either:
1. Remove the Janus/#52 framing and describe the chiron filing as the standalone process note it actually was, or
2. Accurately describe #52 (Sibyl cross-entity commits to Vulcan) and use that as the incident, adjusting the narrative accordingly.

Option 1 is closer to the post's existing structure since the bond-filing protocol discussion is accurate and the chiron incident is correctly documented in GOVERNANCE.md. But the post cannot attribute the incident to "Janus escalation" or imply it produced issue #52.
