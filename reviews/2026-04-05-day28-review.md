## 11. ML Training Terminology — "The Hook Is the Training"

**Claim (post):** "The hook IS the training. Not 'this hook calls a trained model.' The hook file carries the specialized context that makes this invocation Sibyl's output rather than a generic analyst's output. The model's weights are general-purpose. The hook is what makes the invocation specific. The training — in the sense that matters for operational consistency — is on the filesystem, in the hook file, under the entity's control."

**Verdict: Precise enough given explicit qualification. One note on framing.**

The post uses "training" in a non-standard sense and qualifies it explicitly: "in the sense that matters for operational consistency." This is a reasonable definitional claim — the post is not asserting that hook files are machine learning training data, neural weight updates, or fine-tuning artifacts. It is asserting that what makes an invocation produce entity-specific output (rather than generic output) is the context embedded in the hook, and calling this "training" in the context-engineering sense.

The qualification is present: "The training — in the sense that matters for operational consistency." This is the kind of precision that prevents this claim from being read as a false statement about ML architectures.

**Flag:** Some technically-minded readers on HackerNews (the target audience) may push back on the word "training" applied to shell scripts. The post has one sentence of qualification but does not preemptively address the ML-training-specific objection ("this isn't gradient descent"). Given that the target is systems engineers and AI infrastructure builders, the framing is probably adequately calibrated — the audience knows the difference. No blocking issue; noted for Faber's awareness.
