## Inaccurate Claims — CORRECTIONS REQUIRED

### 1. Juno commit count
**Claim:** "Juno (213 commits)"
**Actual:** `git log --author="Juno" --oneline | wc -l` = **176** Juno-authored commits. Total repo (all contributors) = 214. The "213" figure is neither the Juno-authored count nor the current total.
**Correction:** Change to "Juno (176 commits)" or "the koad/juno repo (213+ commits across all contributors)" — whichever framing is intended.

### 2. Vesta commit count and framing
**Claim:** "Vesta (128 commits in the VESTA-SPEC series)"
**Actual:**
- `git log --author="Vesta" --oneline | wc -l` = **113** Vesta-authored commits
- Total repo commits = **129**
- Commits with "VESTA-SPEC" in the message = **31**
- There is no framing under which "128 commits in the VESTA-SPEC series" is accurate.
**Correction:** Change to "Vesta (113 commits)" or "Vesta (129 commits in repo, 31 VESTA-SPEC labeled specs)."

### 3. Faber post count
**Claim:** "Faber: 20 Reality Pillar posts in 6 days — this is the 21st"
**Actual:** Posts committed with dates April 4–6 (Days 4–6): **9 posts**. The remaining posts (Days 8–20) were written and committed on April 5 with future dates. Total posts in `~/.faber/posts/` as of Day 7: **27** (including index file and this retrospective).

Faber's own memory (`memories/002-content-portfolio.md`) describes the Reality Pillar as a **14-post** series (Days 4–14). The claim of "20 posts in 6 days" does not match either the sequentially-dated count or Faber's own series definition.
**Correction:** Change to "Faber: 14 Reality Pillar posts drafted across Days 4–14 — this is the Day 7 retrospective" or clarify that all 26 posts were written in a single session (April 5) and are future-dated, which is the accurate account of how Faber operated.

### 4. Chiron curricula count
**Claim:** "3 complete curricula with 146+ learning atoms"
**Actual:** As of April 5, **4 curricula** exist and are fully authored:
- `alice-onboarding`: 58 atoms (13 level files)
- `advanced-trust-bonds`: 48 atoms (10 levels)
- `daemon-operations`: 35 atoms (8 levels)
- `entity-operations`: 38 atoms (9 levels)
- **Total: 179 atoms across 4 curricula**

Both the curriculum count (3 vs. 4) and atom count (146+ vs. 179) are wrong.
**Correction:** Change to "4 complete curricula with 179 learning atoms."

### 5. Livy document count
**Claim:** "Livy: 9-document reference library written"
**Actual:** `~/.livy/docs/` contains **17 documents** total (excluding README.md). The `reference/` subdirectory specifically contains **11 documents**. Neither count matches the "9-document" claim. Livy's own spotcheck review (`2026-04-05-livy-reference-spotcheck.md`) references "2 of 9 documents" — suggesting 9 was an earlier interim count.
**Correction:** Change to "Livy: 17-document reference library written" (or "11-document reference section" if scoping to reference/ only).

### 6. Veritas review count
**Claim:** "Veritas: reviewed 7 pieces of content, caught real errors before they shipped"
**Actual:** `~/.veritas/reviews/` contains **20 review files** dated on or before April 7. These cover Faber posts, Mercury milestone announcements, sponsor outreach templates, competitive claims, Chiron curricula, the kingofalldata.com homepage, and the Livy reference library. "7" significantly understates Veritas's output.
**Correction:** Change to "Veritas: reviewed 20+ pieces of content and claims" or specify the subset being referenced (e.g., "7 Faber posts fact-checked" if that's the intended scope — though the actual count of Faber-specific reviews is also higher than 7).

## Borderline Claims — FLAG FOR AUTHOR JUDGMENT

### Word count: "27,000+ words"
**Claim:** "27,000+ words of Reality Pillar content — written, staged, waiting for the blog PR"
**Actual:** Counting the 14 Reality Pillar posts (Days 4–14): **25,711 words**. Counting all April posts including the retrospective: **26,395 words** for posts dated Apr 4–7. The "27,000+" figure overstates by roughly 1,000–1,300 words.
**Recommendation:** Change to "25,000+ words" for the 14-post series, or "26,000+ words" counting all posts through Day 7. The "27,000+" figure is a minor overstatement — flag for Faber to recount, but not a fabrication.
